# Systemic Tau (τ_s)

## Learning goals
- State the scientific question that τ_s answers (relational reorganization).
- Distinguish τ_s from a static rank correlation.
- Place τ_s relative to classical EWS (variance, AR1).

## Core definition
**Systemic Tau (τ_s)** is a sliding-window measure of **ordinal coupling** among channels of a multivariate series. In the educational core of STP it is estimated as the mean pairwise Kendall-τ inside each window of length *W* (stride *s*).

The object of study is not “how much variance?” but **how relations reorder** as the system evolves — including across an event or regime change.

## What τ_s is not
- Not a clinical diagnosis or trading signal.
- Not Transfer Entropy (directional information flow).
- Not a static correlation table: the *dynamics* of coupling matter.

## Dual panel
Always read τ_s together with RECD/excess3 and classical EWS. Concordance strengthens a relational claim; discordance demands context.
